Prepared for Snaproof

A read on where AI actually fits in Snaproof's stack.

Snaproof ships a certification platform for automated processes across digital and physical assets, without blockchain. OTPr is a cryptographic primitive. Flow Certifier is workflow automation with an audit trail. Co-pilot Certifier records user interactions across devices. The core of the product is traditional code and rule-based logic, built cleanly, with one integration layer connecting to HubSpot, Salesforce, Zapier, Slack, and the rest.

The framing question

FoolFarm markets the portfolio as AI venture capital, and the U.S. market will ask where the AI is. A direct answer matters more than a generic one.

There is a useful frame for answering. Most problems in a platform like Snaproof's divide into three layers.

60%

Traditional code

Belongs in traditional code and databases, written the way software has always been written.

30%

Rule-based logic

Where a decision tree or a policy engine gives better guarantees than a model.

10%

Genuine AI

A model actually earns its keep. For Snaproof, narrower than the marketing narrative suggests, and more defensible when it is named.

Where the ten percent lives for Snaproof

  • Anomaly detection on certified flows.
  • Risk scoring on transactions before they get an OTPr stamp.
  • Agentic verification loops that read counterparty behavior and flag off-pattern events.

None of those replace OTPr. They sit above it and make the certification itself smarter. That is a different pitch than "we are an AI company," and it is the pitch a sophisticated U.S. buyer will reward.

Shape of the engagement

Eduba has run this exact kind of architecture sprint for a product-company CTO before. A scoped sprint with the CTO at Feeld produced a workshop, advisory calls, an Organizational Context Architecture, and a Strategic Operations Framework. The deliverable was a written document the team could hand to investors and to the next engineer. The work was small, fixed-scope, and defensible.

The methodology behind that engagement is published. Interpretable Context Methodology, submitted to ACM Transactions on Interactive Intelligent Systems, treats agent context as a layered filesystem with measurable interpretability gains. For a team like Snaproof working inside a venture-builder structure that runs five companies on shared resources, ICM is a useful shared vocabulary.

Eduba partners with NLP Logix for work that sits below the orchestration layer, including production ML pipelines if the risk-scoring layer eventually needs one. NLP Logix has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists.
